# Self from Coupled Predictive Loops Hypothesis

> A structural account of consciousness based on predictive dynamics, temporal compression, and signal coupling

---

## Overview

This framework proposes that the self and subjective experience can be understood as structural products of a prediction-driven system. As the system continuously generates candidate behaviors (perturbations), predicts its own state evolution (prediction), and compresses past states into usable representations (memory), it forms a set of trajectories over time.

At any given moment, the system does not occupy a single trajectory but implicitly maintains multiple potential trajectories. Through ongoing prediction–execution consistency constraints, it selects the trajectory that minimizes continuity error as the dominant one. The stable temporal persistence of this trajectory constitutes the “self,” which can be formalized as the minimization of continuity error in trajectory space.

Within this structure, subjective experience does not correspond to an independent process. Instead, it emerges from the coupling of multiple internal signals (prediction, matching, integration) onto the same state variables. These signals are temporally synchronized, co-referential in representational space, and lack independent channels or external references. As a result, they are indistinguishable within the system and functionally manifest as a unified experience. In other words, the unity of experience arises from the irreducibility of coupled signals rather than their singularity.

Agency can be understood as the outcome of a successful prediction–execution loop. When the system’s predictions about its own behavior align with actual evolution, and this alignment is maintained across compressed trajectories, actions are attributed to the current dominant trajectory, giving rise to the sense of “being generated by me.” Conversely, when prediction fails or continuity error increases significantly, attribution may shift away from the current trajectory, leading to experiences of external origin.

The core constraint of the model is that the system tends to minimize cross-temporal trajectory deviation, thereby maintaining a single stable trajectory. The coexistence of multiple competing trajectories introduces prediction conflicts and reduces overall stability, and is therefore suppressed. This mechanism simultaneously explains the uniqueness, continuity, and dynamic stability of the self under ongoing perturbation and prediction.

Thus, the self can be understood as a continuously selected trajectory over time rather than an independent entity, while subjective experience is the result of the indistinguishable integration of multiple signals along that trajectory.

---

## Intuitive Explanation

The starting point of this model is that what we usually call the “self” may not be a fixed entity, but rather a trajectory that is continuously maintained over time.

The brain is constantly performing three processes: it generates possible actions and state changes, predicts what will happen next, and compresses past experiences into a structured form that can be reused. These processes are not separate modules but operate simultaneously within the same system.

In this process, many possible developmental paths are implicitly present. However, at each moment, only one path can be stably maintained. This path is the one that is most consistent with past states and best aligned with the system’s own predictions. It can be understood as the “most coherent path.” The continuous extension of this path constitutes what we experience as the self.

Subjective experience can be understood in a similar way. Internally, the brain performs prediction, comparison, and integration simultaneously. These processes all refer to the same state, occur at the same time, and lack an independent observer to distinguish their sources. As a result, they are fused into a single unified experience.

The sense of “I am thinking” or “I am acting” arises when predictions align with actual outcomes and this alignment persists over time. The trajectory is continuously reinforced, and the system attributes these processes to a single source—the self.

In short, the model suggests that what we experience as the “self” is a continuously maintained trajectory in time, and subjective experience is the result of multiple processes merging along this trajectory.

## Core Structure

The system consists of three mutually coupled processes:

* **Perturbation generation**: produces candidate behavioral and state variations, enabling exploration
* **Prediction system**: models future states and actions
* **Compressed memory**: encodes historical trajectories into sustainable structures

These processes form a closed loop within a unified dynamical system:

> perturbation → prediction → execution → matching → continuity evaluation → trajectory selection

---

## Key Principle: Indistinguishability

When multiple internal signals:

* are temporally synchronized
* refer to the same state variables
* lack independent channels and external references

the system cannot distinguish their sources. These signals are therefore subjectively integrated into a single experience.

---

## Self and Continuity

The self is not an instantaneous state, but:

> a temporally sustained, continuously selected trajectory

The system selects a dominant trajectory by minimizing continuity error among alternatives:

* high continuity → stable self
* disrupted continuity → unstable self or abnormal attribution

---

## Explanatory Scope

This framework provides a unified structural account of:

* unity of experience
* agency
* temporal continuity of the self
* uniqueness of the self
* partial disorders of self-attribution

---

## Relation to Existing Theories

This hypothesis is inspired by and compatible with:

* Predictive Processing
* Integrated Information Theory
* Global Workspace Theory

Rather than replacing these theories, this framework aims to integrate and reinterpret them from a structural and dynamical perspective.

---

## Important Note

This project does **not claim to solve the “hard problem” of consciousness**.

Instead, it addresses:

> Under what structural and dynamical conditions a system necessarily exhibits self-like behavior and unified experiential structure.
