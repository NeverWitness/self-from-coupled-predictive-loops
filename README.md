# Self from Coupled Predictive Loops Hypothesis
> Self from Coupled Predictive Loops Hypothesis  A structural account of consciousness based on predictive dynamics, temporal compression, and signal coupling.

## Overview

This project proposes a **hypothesis** for the emergence of selfhood, subjective experience, and agency based on the coupling of predictive processes in the brain.

Rather than treating consciousness as a separate entity, this framework suggests that:

> The sense of self emerges from temporally continuous, compressed behavioral trajectories, maintained by tightly coupled predictive, integrative, and matching processes.

---

## Status

This is an **early-stage theoretical hypothesis**.

* Not formally validated
* Not fully formalized
* Intended as an exploratory framework

---

## Core Hypothesis

The model assumes three fundamental functional processes:

1. **Perturbation Generator**
   Introduces variability and exploration into behavior.

2. **Predictive System**
   Continuously predicts future internal states and actions.

3. **Compressed Memory System**
   Maintains a temporally continuous, compressed trajectory of past states.

---

## Key Principle: Indistinguishability

When multiple internal signals:

* Are temporally synchronized
* Refer to the same behavioral variable
* Lack independent sensory channels

They become **subjectively indistinguishable**, resulting in a unified experience.

---

## Emergence of Self

The sense of self arises when:

* Current state is consistent with predicted trajectory
* Temporal continuity is preserved
* Internal signals remain tightly coupled

This produces:

* Unity of experience
* Sense of agency
* Persistence of identity

---

## Explanatory Scope

This framework aims to provide structural explanations for:

* Unity of consciousness
* Sense of agency
* Temporal continuity of self
* Certain pathological disruptions of self-attribution

---

## Relation to Existing Theories

This hypothesis is inspired by and compatible with:

* Predictive Processing
* Integrated Information Theory
* Global Workspace Theory

It does not replace these frameworks, but attempts to integrate and reinterpret them from an information-structural perspective.

---

## Important Note

This project does **not** claim to solve the “hard problem” of consciousness.

Instead, it focuses on:

> The structural and dynamical conditions under which a system necessarily exhibits self-like behavior and unified subjective reports.
