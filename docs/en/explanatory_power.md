# Explanatory Power

## Self from Coupled Predictive Loops Hypothesis

---

## 1. Scope

This document outlines the capacity of the theory to **account for observed phenomena**.

These explanations do not constitute proof, but demonstrate that the framework provides:

* Structural coherence
* Cross-phenomenal unification
* Compatibility with existing research

---

## 2. Unity of Consciousness

### Phenomenon

Subjective experience appears as a single unified field, rather than multiple independent streams.

---

### Explanation

* Multiple internal signals (prediction, matching, integration)
* Are temporally synchronized
* Refer to the same state variables
* Lack independent processing channels

→ These conditions satisfy indistinguishability

Therefore:

> Multiple signals are subjectively fused into a single experience.

---

### Key Mechanisms

* Signal coupling
* Indistinguishability principle

---

## 3. Sense of Thinking

### Phenomenon

Thinking is experienced as a continuous and unified process.

---

### Explanation

Thinking arises from the coupling of three signal classes:

* Prediction (future states)
* Integration (current state positioning)
* Matching (consistency between prediction and actual state)

Because:

* They occur synchronously
* Refer to the same object
* Lack external reference

→ Their sources cannot be distinguished

Therefore:

> They are experienced as “I am thinking”.

---

## 4. Sense of Agency

### Phenomenon

Individuals experience themselves as initiating and controlling actions.

---

### Explanation

The system operates through a closed loop:

```id="0kq9hf"
perturbation → prediction → execution → matching → continuity evaluation
```

When:

* Predictions succeed
* Execution aligns with prediction
* Continuity is preserved

the system attributes:

> The action to itself.

---

### Key Point

Agency is not a primitive; it is:

> The result of successful prediction–matching alignment.

---

## 5. Continuity of Self

### Phenomenon

Individuals experience themselves as the same entity over time.

---

### Explanation

* Historical states are compressed into trajectories
* Current states are matched against predicted trajectories
* Continuity error is minimized

Therefore:

> The current state is interpreted as a continuation of past trajectories.

---

### Core Expression

```id="1z8nrf"
Self = trajectory with minimal continuity error
```

---

## 6. Uniqueness of Self

### Phenomenon

Individuals typically experience only a single self.

---

### Explanation

* Multiple candidate trajectories may exist
* The system selects the one with minimal continuity error

Additionally:

* Multiple trajectories would generate prediction conflicts
* The system tends toward a single stable trajectory

Therefore:

> Only one dominant self is maintained.

---

## 7. Attribution of Action (Self vs External)

### Phenomenon

Individuals can distinguish between:

* Self-generated actions
* Externally caused events

---

### Explanation

* Self-generated actions: prediction succeeds → attributed to self
* External events: prediction fails → attributed to external causes

Therefore:

> Attribution is based on predictive consistency.

---

## 8. Disturbances of Self

### Phenomenon

Under certain conditions, individuals experience:

* Loss of voluntary control
* Externalization of thoughts
* Instability of self

---

### Explanation (Hypothesized)

Possible causes include:

* Prediction failure
* Increased continuity error
* Reduced signal coupling

These lead to:

* Breakdown of matching mechanisms
* Misattribution of actions or thoughts

---

## 9. Emergence of First-Person Perspective

### Phenomenon

Experience is expressed in the form of “I”.

---

### Explanation

Generative pathway:

```id="v3m0cx"
multi-signal coupling
→ indistinguishability
→ unified internal state
→ single output interface
→ “I”
```

Therefore:

> The first-person perspective is an output format, not an independent structure.

---

## 10. Irreducibility of Experience

### Phenomenon

Experience cannot be decomposed into independently accessible components.

---

### Explanation

Due to:

* Absence of independent sensory channels
* Lack of external reference
* Temporal synchrony of signals

The system cannot perform source separation.

Therefore:

> Experience appears as an irreducible whole.

---

## 11. Functional Role of Perturbation (Exploration)

### Phenomenon

Behavior is not fully predictable.

---

### Explanation

* Perturbation generates candidate trajectories
* Provides a space for selection
* Enables adaptability

Therefore:

> Uncertainty is a functional component of the system.

---

## 12. Summary of Explanatory Scope

The model provides a unified account of:

* Unity of experience
* Continuity of self
* Sense of agency
* Uniqueness of self
* Action attribution
* Certain abnormal states

These phenomena no longer require separate explanations, but emerge from a shared structure:

> prediction + compression + coupling + continuity minimization

---

## 13. Limitations

The explanatory scope remains limited in several respects:

* Does not explain why subjective experience exists
* Does not yet provide quantitative predictions
* Lacks empirical validation

---

## 14. Conclusion

The explanatory strength of this framework lies in:

* Explaining multiple phenomena through a unified mechanism
* Avoiding additional ontological assumptions
* Remaining compatible with existing research

Its central claim can be summarized as:

> Self and experience arise from the indistinguishable fusion of multi-source signals along temporally continuous trajectories.
