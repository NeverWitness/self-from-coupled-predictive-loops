# Explanatory Power

> Self from Coupled Predictive Loops Hypothesis

---

## 1. Note

This document demonstrates the theory’s **capacity to explain existing phenomena**.
These explanations do not constitute proof, but aim to show that the framework has:

* Structural consistency
* Cross-phenomenon unification
* Compatibility with existing research

---

## 2. Unity of Consciousness

### Phenomenon

Subjective experience appears as a single, unified whole rather than multiple independent streams.

---

### Explanation in This Model

Multiple internal signals (prediction, matching, state integration) exist within the system. These signals:

* Are highly synchronized in time
* Refer to the same state variables
* Share a representational space
* Lack independent channels or external references

→ Satisfy the conditions for indistinguishability

Therefore:

> Multiple signals collapse into a single state along the dominant trajectory, manifesting as unified experience.

---

### Key Mechanisms

* Signal coupling
* Indistinguishability principle
* Dominant trajectory constraint

---

## 3. Sense of Thinking

### Phenomenon

Thinking is experienced as a continuous and unified process.

---

### Explanation in This Model

Thinking corresponds to state evolution along the dominant trajectory, involving:

* Expansion of multiple predictions
* Integration of current state
* Prediction–evolution matching

Because these processes:

* Occur synchronously
* Refer to the same trajectory state
* Cannot be distinguished in origin

Therefore:

> They are unified and attributed as a single process, experienced as “I am thinking.”

---

## 4. Sense of Agency

### Phenomenon

Individuals experience themselves as actively choosing and controlling actions.

---

### Explanation in This Model

The system generates multiple predictive trajectories, and actual evolution aligns with one of them.

When the following conditions hold:

* Prediction matches actual evolution
* The match persists over time
* The trajectory aligns with the dominant trajectory

Then:

> The current behavior is attributed to the dominant trajectory → experienced as “generated by me.”

If these conditions are not met:

→ Attribution may shift → producing a sense of external origin

---

### Key Point

Agency is fundamentally:

> The result of attribution based on prediction–evolution matching

---

## 5. Continuity of Self

### Phenomenon

Individuals experience themselves as the same person over time.

---

### Explanation in This Model

* Historical states are compressed into trajectory structures
* Current states align with predicted trajectories
* Continuity error is minimized

Therefore:

> The current state is interpreted as a continuation of the same trajectory.

---

### Core Expression

```
Self = trajectory with minimal continuity error
```

---

## 6. Uniqueness of Self

### Phenomenon

Individuals typically experience only a single self.

---

### Explanation in This Model

Although multiple predictive trajectories exist:

* Continuity error cannot be minimized simultaneously across multiple trajectories
* Multiple trajectories introduce prediction conflicts
* The system converges toward a stable solution (a single dominant trajectory)

Therefore:

> Only one trajectory is continuously selected → forming a unique self.

---

## 7. Behavioral Attribution (Self vs External)

### Phenomenon

Individuals can distinguish between:

* Self-generated actions
* Externally caused events

---

### Explanation in This Model

Attribution is based on:

* If actual evolution matches a predictive trajectory → attributed to that trajectory
* If matching fails or becomes discontinuous → attribution shifts

Therefore:

> Whether a behavior is experienced as self-generated depends on matching and continuity.

---

## 8. Disturbances of Self

### Phenomenon

In certain conditions, individuals experience:

* Loss of voluntary control
* Externalization of thoughts
* Instability of self

---

### Explanation in This Model

Possible mechanisms include:

* Failure of prediction–evolution matching
* Increased continuity error
* Instability of the dominant trajectory
* Weakening of signal coupling

These lead to:

* Breakdown or shift in attribution mechanisms
* Instability of self-structure

---

## 9. Emergence of the First-Person Perspective

### Phenomenon

Experience is expressed in the form of “I.”

---

### Explanation in This Model

Generation pathway:

```
Multi-prediction and multi-signal coupling
→ Indistinguishability
→ Formation of dominant trajectory
→ Single internal representation
→ Output interface
→ “I”
```

Therefore:

> The first-person perspective is a structural output, not an independent entity.

---

## 10. Why Experience Is Indivisible

### Phenomenon

Individuals cannot decompose experience into multiple sources.

---

### Explanation in This Model

Because:

* Signals share a representational space
* There are no independent channels
* There is no external reference
* Signals are highly synchronized in time

The system cannot perform source separation:

> Multiple signals collapse into a single representational state.

Thus, experience appears as an indivisible whole.

---

## 11. Uncertainty and Multi-Prediction

### Phenomenon

Behavior exhibits partial indeterminacy and flexibility.

---

### Explanation in This Model

The system inherently has a multi-prediction structure:

* Generates multiple possible trajectories simultaneously
* Models different potential scenarios
* Retains uncertainty during selection

Therefore:

> Uncertainty arises from the multi-prediction structure, not necessarily from external perturbations.

---

## 12. Unified Explanatory Power

This model explains, through a unified mechanism:

* Unity of experience
* Continuity of self
* Agency (as attribution mechanism)
* Uniqueness of self
* Behavioral attribution
* Disturbances of self

All of these phenomena arise from:

> Multi-prediction structure + trajectory selection + indistinguishability + continuity constraints

---

## 13. Limitations

The current explanatory scope still has limitations:

* Does not address why subjective experience exists (the hard problem)
* Continuity error is not fully formalized
* Lacks quantitative predictions
* Lacks experimental validation

---

## 14. Summary

The explanatory strength of this model lies in:

* Using a single structural mechanism to explain multiple classes of phenomena
* Avoiding the introduction of additional entities
* Maintaining compatibility with existing cognitive science frameworks

Its core can be summarized as:

> The self is a trajectory continuously selected within a multi-prediction system;
> subjective experience is the indistinguishable integration of multiple signals along that trajectory;
> and agency is an attribution mechanism based on sustained prediction–evolution matching.
